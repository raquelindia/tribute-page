TERENCE MCKENNA

American Ethnobotanist


    Here is a timeline of Terence Mckenna's life:

  • Born: November 16th, 1946

  • 1965: McKenna enrolled in the University of California, Berkeley.

  • 1967: Discovered and began studying shamanism.

  • 1969: Became a professional butterfly collector in Indonesia.

  • 1971: Mckenna's mother dies from cancer, he goes on to search for DMT in the Columbian Amazon.

  • 1972: Mckenna returns to U.C. Berkeley to finish his studies.

  • 1975: Mckenna graduates with a degree in ecology, shamanism, and conservation of natural resources.

  • 1976: Mckenna and his brother published a book entitled Psilocybin: Magic Mushroom Grower's Guide.

  • Early 1980s: Mckenna starts to publicly speak on the topic of psychadelic drugs.

  • 1985: McKenna founded Botanical Dimensions with his then-wife, Kathleen Harrison.

  • Early 1990s: McKenna also became a popular personality in the psychedelic rave/dance scene.

  • 1994: Mckenna appeared as a speaker at the Starwood Festival, documented in the book Tripping by Charles Hayes.

  • May 22nd, 1999: Mckenna starts to suffer from extreme headaches and is diagnosed with brain cancer.

  • Died: April 3rd, 2000.
